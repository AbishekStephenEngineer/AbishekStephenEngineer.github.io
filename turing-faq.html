FAQ on working with Turing - Most frequently asked questions and questions that are already answered in Turing's FAQ that nobody reads even after I share it with them

This is not the official FAQ page of Turing. This is a compilation of questions frequently asked on my LinkedIn messages. Turing FAQ can be found here. If there's any question unanswered in the Turing FAQ page and on this page, feel free to ask in LinkedIn message. Page last updated 16-Nov-2024

  1. What is Turing?

    Go to Turing.com to learn about Turing. They have tie ups with tech giants like Nvidia, Microsoft, OpenAI and Google!

  2. Is Turing legit?

    It depends. Check if your contact sends email from domain ending in turing.com. If you are getting interviewed by any other platform, do your own research and don't get scammed by people posing as Turing employees. Turing never asks for money in any case whatsoever.

  3. What are the types of jobs available at Turing?

    You can join as a contractor (working for one of the clients of Turing) or as a member of the core team (employee of Turing). I am currently a contractor and have no idea about the core team hiring process or on campus recruitment process.

  4. How is the work culture at Turing?

    I am working as a contractor and for one of the clients of Turing. I do not work as a core member. I have no idea if you are joining as a contractor or a core member. If you are asking about my client, that is classified and I don't know who your client is or what their work culture is like. If you are joining as a core member, I am a contractor and I have no idea about FTEs.

  5. Can you refer me to this job opening at Turing? Please share HR contact/Manager contact/CEO contact (really? someone did ask me this in a cold message)

    There is no such direct referral at Turing. Even if there was, I am not going to reccommend a total stranger, even if they are a knight or a dragon or a wizard at leetcode/hackersomething or whatever. I only have a new registeration referral link and you have to complete the assessments like everyone.

  6. Does Turing provide laptop for work?

    For contractros, no. You are expected to bring your own gear. For FTE, I don't know.

  7. I have doubt about on campus interview

    I signed up and took the interview online. I have no idea what on campus interview process is like. Contact your placement cell or POC from Turing to get your doubts cleared.

  8. Can I really get job as a fresher?

    I know 3 pre-final year and final year students who started working in Turing. Note that it depends on market demand/how well you advertise yourself and your work in tech.

  9. I have sent you a message 3 days ago like "Hi", "Hello" or "Hi Abishek, It's great connecting with you. How have you been?" Why didn't you respond?

    Have you tried telling me what you actually want instead of waiting for me to reply "Hi" to 100 new connections I get everyday? No? Then this page might be helpful.

  10. I have trouble on boarding with Turing

    Contact Turing support. I am not Turing support.

  11. Do they fire frequently?

    It is like any IT job, your contract can get terminated if the company and/or project is not doing well. Turing's promise is that they will get your next contract ready asap if your current contract expires. Read Turing's FAQ for more details.

  12. Is contract work good? I have only worked as an FTE till now

    I have over 8 years of experience in tech and about 4.5 of it as an FTE, 1.5 in my own startup and 2 in Turing as a contractor. After only 2 years I can assure you that I am never going back to FTE role. One of the major advantage of working as a contractor is the amount you save in tax. Look up 44ADA presumptive taxation of Income Tax Act and Letter Of Undertaking (LUT) of GST act. As an FTE, when you earn in lakhs per month you will pay tax at the rate of ~40% or more but you only pay ~10% as a contractor. I can work from anywhere, stay out of over priced and over populated big cities and save/invest almost all of my income.

  13. Do we need to work from office? Where is Turing office located?

    Turing has only permenant remote jobs that you have to work from home/anywhere. Turin'g office is in California, you don't have to go there in any case.

  20. I have completed all assessments. How long will I wait? How to get selected faster?

    Upload screenshots of your software/projects under each project you list in your Turing Developer profile. Simple as that. But it depends on market demand as well, so, God's grace and luck plays a part as well I guess?

  21. How long does it take to get hired?

    As mentioned in their FAQ, it takes around 10 days or 2 weeks, but again, it depends on market demand and your performance.

  24. Are there any deductions in pay?

    Nope. You get what you are contracted for and exactly for the hours you work. There could be forex and/or processing fee if you are cahing in to your bank account in your country's currency from USD, Otherwise, nope.

  25. Do we get any employee benefits like dental/medical/insurance/pension?

    As a contractor, afaik, nope. For FTE I don't know as I am a contractor.

  26. How many assessments should I take? How long should I perpare?

    If you fail and assessment, you can try after 3 months. If you pass but didn't get 5 starts in the assessment (oh yeah, there's a grading system for the assessment as well. You can't see it, but the managers can in your Turing Developer profile), you can't reattempt it and you are stuck. I'd wager hiring managers tend to pick the best of the best, so prepare well for each assessment. Take your time. Do not rush. Take as many assessments you can to better your chances of getting hired. I have cleared around 12 assessments and all of them are 5 stars.
